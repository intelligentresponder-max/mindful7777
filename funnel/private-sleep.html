01

Why sleep breaks down

Understanding before fixing

Most sleep problems are not problems with sleep. They are problems with the transition — the gap between doing and being, between the day-self and the night-self. The mind has not been given permission to stop.

Hypnosis works here because it operates precisely in that gap. It is, by definition, the art of transitioning states — from waking to trance, from tension to release, from thinking to simply being.

Your first night — tonight

Before sleep: sit on the edge of the bed. Take 3 slow breaths. Say (silently or aloud): "The day is complete. I did enough. I am allowed to rest." Repeat until you mean it. Then lie down. That's all.

03

The Hypnosis Anchor

A tool you keep forever

By night 4, you will create a personal anchor — a physical gesture paired with a hypnotic state — that you can use any time, any place, to access deep relaxation in seconds. This is one of the most practical tools hypnosis offers, and it is yours permanently once installed.

How anchoring works

While deeply relaxed, touch your thumb and ring finger together. Hold for 30 seconds while noticing the depth of your calm. Release. Repeat. After 4-7 repetitions across different sessions, that touch becomes a direct trigger to the state. Your nervous system learns the shortcut.
